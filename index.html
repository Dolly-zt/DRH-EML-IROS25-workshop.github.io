Dexterous Robotic Hand with Embodied Manipulation Learning​

DRH-EML

About DRH-EML

Human-like manipulation ability is essential for robots, in which dexterous robotic hand (DRH) is the ideal end-effector to implement human-like manipulation ability. However, how to align the 20s Degrees-of-Freedoms (DoFs) of a human hand in limited space is very challenging for existing mechatronic systems. Currently, the dexterous hands can be categories into two types: the fully-actuated ones and the under-actuated ones. The fully-actuated dexterous hands, taking the Shadow hand as example, achieve good functionality. But the size and weight of its driven box brings negative effector to the manipulator. While the under-actuated dexterous hands, taking the BrainCo Hand as example, achieve a good balance between functionality and complexity. But its performance in complex manipulation is limited. Except for the mechanical design, the dexterous robotic hands also encounter bottleneck in large-area and high-resolution tactile sensing, which is very important for interacting with the physical world. With the development of embodied manipulation learning, the problem that what kind of embodiment is the most suitable is still unknown to researchers. In this workshop, we would like to bring together the world wide experts working in DRH and embodied manipulation learning. We would like to discuss the current state-of-the-arts in the dexterous hand, including the design, control, perception, and embodied learning for dexterous robotic hand. We aim to conclude the existing challenges for the further development of DRH and predict the potential technologies, e.g., artificial muscles/skins, to address these challenges.
